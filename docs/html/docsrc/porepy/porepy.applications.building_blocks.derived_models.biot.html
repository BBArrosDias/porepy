porepy.applications.building_blocks.derived_models.biot module

Model class for poromechanical equations under the Biot assumptions [1, 2, 3].

This class can be seen as a subset of the full poromechanical system of equations, where certain simplifications are introduced so that the classical system of equations following Biot’s consolidation theory is recovered.

In particular, we set the fluid density as constant by requiring the fluid compressibility \(c_f\) to be zero,

\[\]

ho := ho_0 exp{c_f (p - p_0)} = ho_0,

and define the poromechanics porosity as:

\[\phi(p, \mathbf{u}) := \phi_0 + S_\]

where :math:` ho_0`, \(\phi_0\), and \(p_0\) are the reference density, porosity, and pressure, and \(S_ arepsilon\), \(lpha\), \(p\), and \(\mathbf{u}\), are the specific storage, Biot’s coefficient, pressure, and displacement, respectively.

Note, however, that the above simplifications do not reflect the actual _physical_ assumptions considered in Biot’s theory. e.g., the fluid is actually slightly compressible rather than incompressible. Thus, the above simplifications must be seen as implementation shortcuts rather than proper physical assumptions.

where \(\sigma\) is the poroelastic (total) stress, \(\mathbf{F}\) is a vector source (usually a body force), \(\mathcal{C}\) is the stiffness matrix ( written in terms of Lamé parameters in the case of isotropic solids), and \(\mathbf{I}\) is the identity matrix. In the mass conservation equation, \(\mathbf{q}\) is the Darcy flux, and \(f\) is an external source of fluid mass. Finally, \(\mathcal{K}\) and \(\mu_f\) denote the intrinsic permeability and the fluid dynamic viscosity.
